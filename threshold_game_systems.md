### Backgrounds, Derived Stats, and Combat Loop

## PART 1: BACKGROUNDS (5)

Backgrounds represent who you were before threading — your place in the old world, the skills you carried into the new one, and the ghosts that followed you. They are selected at character creation alongside race (Baseline or Threaded) and class (primer sequence). Each background provides a minor mechanical bonus, a starting knowledge advantage, and a unique quest chain that feeds into the larger world narrative.

Backgrounds are permanent. Like primer sequences, they shape your cost gradient — not by changing affinity tiers, but by providing persistent passive bonuses that subtly reward certain playstyles.

---

### 1. SUBLEVEL RESIDENT
*You grew up below the line. Not the comfortable sublevels with working air recyclers and Directorate maintenance contracts — the deep ones, where the lights flicker and the infrastructure runs on stubbornness and stolen parts.*

**Who you were:** A laborer, a fixer, a survivor. You know the sublevels the way rats know walls — every service corridor, every dead zone where the scanners don't reach, every shortcut that the Directorate's maps don't show. You've breathed bad air your whole life and your lungs have the scars to prove it, but you can navigate industrial wreckage blindfolded and you know how to make broken things work long enough to matter.

**Mechanical bonus:** +1 Vigor, +5% bonus to Scavenging and Engineering XP gains.

**Starting knowledge:** You begin the game with a partial map of Sublevel infrastructure — hidden passages, scanner dead zones, and maintenance access points are revealed in your starting zone. Other players must discover these through exploration or Lockpicking.

**Unique quest chain:** *The Deep Vent* — An old service network beneath Sublevel Twenty has been sealed since before the Threshold Act. Something down there is generating heat signatures that don't match any known biological or mechanical source. You know the access route because your mother used it to smuggle medical supplies when you were a child. Now the Unbound wants it opened, and the Directorate wants it sealed permanently. The chain explores what was locked away and why — and whether some doors were closed for good reason.

### 2. LAPSED CLERK
*You worked for the Directorate. Not the glamorous parts — not AGS or the HIB. You were administrative. Forms, registrations, civic status processing. You saw the system from inside the machine, and what you saw made you walk away.*

**Who you were:** A bureaucrat who understood how the Directorate's power actually operates — not through force, but through paperwork. You know how civic status classifications work at the database level, how helix registration records are structured, how enforcement orders are routed and approved. You also know where the system's gaps are, because you spent years watching people fall through them.

**Mechanical bonus:** +1 Presence, +5% bonus to Negotiation and Genomics XP gains.

**Starting knowledge:** You begin the game with detailed knowledge of Directorate civic procedures. NPC interactions with government officials offer additional dialogue options — you know the right forms to cite, the right language to use, and the right questions to ask. Directorate-aligned quest givers offer you missions earlier than they would other players.

**Unique quest chain:** *The Bleached File* — Before you left, you noticed a pattern in the civic status records: a category of citizens whose files had been systematically sanitized. Not deleted — bleached. Their records existed but contained no meaningful data. Every bleached file shared one characteristic: the individual had been flagged for high Phlux during routine medical screening, then... nothing. No follow-up. No enforcement action. No re-registration. They simply vanished from the system without vanishing from the world. The chain investigates what the Directorate does with high-Phlux individuals it identifies but doesn't arrest — and what those individuals are being used for.

### 3. STREET MEDIC
*Before threading, you kept people alive with expired antibiotics, salvaged surgical tools, and a willingness to learn from your mistakes — because your patients couldn't afford the alternative.*

**Who you were:** An unlicensed medical practitioner serving communities that the Directorate's healthcare system classifies as "non-priority" — Conditional citizens, undocumented residents, people whose civic status doesn't qualify them for a hospital bed. You learned medicine from stolen textbooks and practiced it in kitchens and backrooms. You've set bones with cable ties and treated infections with cleaning solution because that's what was available. You're not a doctor. You're what people get instead of a doctor when the system decides they don't deserve one.

**Mechanical bonus:** +1 Resolve, +5% bonus to Mending and Alchemy XP gains.

**Starting knowledge:** You begin the game with the ability to craft basic medical consumables (Tier 1 healing stims) without investing skill points in Alchemy. This advantage disappears once other players reach Alchemy 5, but gives you early-game utility. You also recognize symptoms of expression cascading — if a nearby player or NPC is suffering from threading complications, you receive a notification that other players don't.

**Unique quest chain:** *The Quiet Ward* — A Baseline community in the Pale has been experiencing a cluster of identical symptoms: tremors, bioluminescent flashes beneath the skin, and intermittent electromagnetic perception. None of these people have been threaded. The symptoms appeared spontaneously, starting six weeks ago, spreading outward from the community's water reclamation point. You're the only person they trust enough to let investigate. The chain explores the possibility of environmental threading — triplex formation triggered not by a primer but by contaminated infrastructure — and whether it's accidental or deliberate.

### 4. SALVAGE RUNNER
*The Strand doesn't waste anything. Everything that breaks gets stripped, everything stripped gets rebuilt, and everything rebuilt gets sold. You were part of that cycle — the person who went into the dangerous places to pull out the valuable pieces.*

**Who you were:** An independent operator in the Strand's salvage economy. You navigated condemned structures, decommissioned industrial zones, and the occasional Directorate-quarantined site to recover materials that the legitimate supply chain couldn't or wouldn't touch. You've been irradiated twice, chemically burned once, and arrested four times. You know every fence, every broker, and every back-channel buyer between Sublevel Six and the Strand's outer perimeter. You also know which buyers ask questions and which ones don't.

**Mechanical bonus:** +1 Acuity, +5% bonus to Scavenging and Smithing XP gains.

**Starting knowledge:** You begin the game with an expanded vendor network — black-market merchants in the Strand and lower sublevels are available to you from the start. Other players must unlock these through faction standing or quest completion. You also start with a slightly larger inventory (one additional row) reflecting a career spent carrying heavy loads through tight spaces.

**Unique quest chain:** *The Graveyard Orbit* — A decommissioned atmospheric processing station in the Strand's outer zone has been marked for demolition, but the demolition keeps getting delayed. Officially, structural instability. Unofficially, your contacts say there's something inside that multiple factions want — the Directorate, the Unbound, and a third party that nobody can identify. You've been inside the station before, years ago, when it was just a ruin. It wasn't empty then either. The chain involves navigating competing faction interests to reach the station's core and discovering what it contains: an intact, pre-Threshold AGS research archive that predates the Kowloon Paper — evidence that the Directorate knew about the third strand before the public did.

### 5. DISGRACED ACADEMIC
*You had a lab. You had funding. You had a career trajectory that pointed toward tenure and comfortable irrelevance. Then you published something the Directorate didn't want published, and all of it disappeared overnight.*

**Who you were:** A researcher — biology, genomics, biophysics, or a related field. You weren't working on threading directly, but your work brushed close enough to the classified perimeter that AGS took notice. Maybe you identified anomalous methylation patterns in population-level genomic data. Maybe you published a theoretical framework for targeted epigenetic rewriting that was a little too close to what AGS was actually doing. Whatever it was, your funding was revoked, your institutional access was terminated, and your name was added to a list that makes it difficult to get hired anywhere that requires a background check.

**Mechanical bonus:** +1 Cortex, +5% bonus to Genomics and Strand Weaving XP gains.

**Starting knowledge:** You begin the game with the ability to identify the primer sequence and approximate Phlux coefficient of NPCs and players by visual observation (a simplified version of the Genomics "Gene Read" ability). This passive identification doesn't require interaction — you can assess targets at range. You also start with one additional Strand Weaving or Helix Assault recipe (player's choice) reflecting theoretical knowledge that hasn't yet been put into practice.

**Unique quest chain:** *The Nakamura Correspondence* — Before the Threshold Act, you exchanged three emails with Dr. Yuki Nakamura — the architect of PNA-based triplex therapy — about a discrepancy in her published frataxin recovery data. The numbers didn't add up. Not because they were wrong, but because they were incomplete — Nakamura had observed a secondary effect during her trials that she deliberately excluded from the published results. She hinted at it in her last email to you before going silent. The chain follows the trail of that secondary effect through archived communications, abandoned research sites, and the people Nakamura trusted before the Directorate absorbed her work. What you discover connects directly to the Recurrence Signal — and suggests that Nakamura heard it first.

## PART 2: DERIVED STATS

These formulas convert the 8 core attributes into gameplay numbers. All values use a base + scaling model. Base values ensure that even a character with minimal investment in an attribute has functional stats. Scaling values reward specialization without creating exponential gaps.

### Primary Resources

**Hit Points (HP)**
```
HP = 100 + (Vigor × 15) + (Soma × 5) + (Level × 10)
```
- Vigor is the primary driver. A Level 1 character with 10 Vigor and 10 Soma has 350 HP.
- Soma contributes a secondary bonus reflecting the correlation between physical mass and damage absorption.
- Level scaling ensures HP grows meaningfully with progression.

**Strand (Resource Pool)**
```
Strand = 50 + (Cortex × 12) + (Resolve × 3) + (Level × 5)
```
- Cortex is the primary driver. A Level 1 Catalyst with 13 Cortex has 221 Strand.
- Resolve contributes a minor buffer reflecting mental stability's role in sustaining Expression channeling.
- Strand regenerates passively at a rate of (Cortex × 0.5) per tick (1 tick = 3 seconds).

**Stamina**
```
Stamina = 80 + (Vigor × 8) + (Soma × 4) + (Level × 6)
```
- Used for physical abilities (Melee Combat, Athletics, Defense, Martial Forms).
- Regenerates passively at (Vigor × 0.8) per tick.
- Stamina and Strand are separate pools: physical classes spend Stamina, Expression classes spend Strand, hybrid builds manage both.

### Offensive Stats

**Melee Damage**
```
Base Melee Damage = Weapon Damage + (Soma × 2.0)
```
- Flat scaling. Every point of Soma adds 2 damage to melee attacks.
- Critical hits multiply this by the critical damage multiplier (see below).

**Expression Damage**
```
Base Expression Damage = Ability Base Damage + (Cortex × 2.5)
```
- Cortex scales Expression damage more steeply than Soma scales melee, reflecting the glass cannon tradeoff — Catalysts hit harder but can't take hits.

**Ranged Damage**
```
Base Ranged Damage = Weapon Damage + (Soma × 1.0) + (Acuity × 1.0)
```
- Split scaling: raw force (Soma) and precision (Acuity) contribute equally.

**Critical Strike Chance**
```
Crit Chance = 5% + (Reflex × 0.5%) + (Phlux × 0.3%)
```
- Base 5% ensures everyone crits sometimes.
- At 20 Reflex and 10 Phlux, crit chance is 18% — meaningful but not dominant.
- Capped at 40% to prevent degenerate builds.

**Critical Damage Multiplier**
```
Crit Multiplier = 150% + (Phlux × 3%)
```
- Base crits do 150% damage.
- At 15 Phlux, crits do 195% damage. High-Phlux characters swing harder on crits but are less consistent overall.
- Capped at 250%.

### Defensive Stats

**Armor Rating**
```
Armor = Equipment Armor + (Vigor × 1.5)
```
- Damage reduction from armor follows diminishing returns:
```
Damage Reduction = Armor / (Armor + 200 + (Attacker Level × 10))
```
- This ensures armor is always valuable but never provides immunity. At 300 Armor vs. a Level 30 attacker, reduction is ~37%.

**Dodge Chance**
```
Dodge Chance = (Reflex × 0.6%) + (Acuity × 0.2%)
```
- At 20 Reflex and 15 Acuity, dodge is 15%.
- Capped at 30%. Dodge avoids the full hit — it's binary, not partial.

**Crowd Control Resistance**
```
CC Resistance = (Resolve × 1.5%) + (Vigor × 0.5%)
```
- Reduces duration of stuns, silences, slows, and roots by this percentage.
- At 15 Resolve and 10 Vigor, CC duration is reduced by 27.5%.

### Utility Stats

**Movement Speed**
```
Movement Speed = Base Speed × (1 + Reflex × 0.01)
```
- Each point of Reflex adds 1% movement speed. At 20 Reflex, you move 20% faster than base.

**Inventory Capacity**
```
Inventory Slots = 20 + floor(Soma / 3)
```
- Base 20 slots. Every 3 Soma adds 1 slot. At 15 Soma, you have 25 slots.

**Healing Received**
```
Healing Modifier = 1.0 + (Resolve × 0.02)
```
- Each point of Resolve increases healing received by 2%.
- At 15 Resolve, healing received is 130% effective.

**Gathering Yield**
```
Yield Modifier = 1.0 + (Acuity × 0.03)
```
- Each point of Acuity increases materials gathered by 3%. At 15 Acuity, yield is 145%.

**Vendor Price Modifier**
```
Buy Price = Base Price × (1 - Presence × 0.01)
Sell Price = Base Price × (0.3 + Presence × 0.01)
```
- Each Presence point reduces buy cost by 1% and increases sell value by 1%.
- At 15 Presence, you buy at 85% and sell at 45% of base.

**Faction Reputation Gain**
```
Rep Modifier = 1.0 + (Presence × 0.02)
```
- Each Presence point adds 2% to faction reputation earned.

**Loot Quality Roll**
```
Loot Bonus = Phlux × 0.5%
```
- Each point of Phlux adds 0.5% to the chance of a loot drop being upgraded one rarity tier.
- At 15 Phlux, there's a 7.5% chance any drop upgrades. Small but felt over time.

**Death Penalty Modifier**
```
Base Penalty = XP Loss % + Resource Loss %
Phlux Variance = ±(Phlux × 0.5%)
```
- High Phlux makes death penalties *variable* — sometimes lighter, sometimes heavier than expected. This is the attribute's signature: unpredictability in both directions.

### Strand Regeneration and Tick Rate

```
Strand Regen = (Cortex × 0.5) per tick
Stamina Regen = (Vigor × 0.8) per tick
HP Regen (out of combat) = (Vigor × 1.0) per tick
HP Regen (in combat) = (Vigor × 0.2) per tick
Tick Rate = 1 tick per 3 seconds
```

At character creation, players distribute points across 8 attributes from a pool.

```
Base per attribute: 5
Bonus points from class: 8 (distributed per class table)
Free points to allocate: 10
Minimum per attribute: 3
Maximum per attribute at creation: 18
```

This means a Level 1 Sentinel who puts all 10 free points into Soma would have:
- Soma: 5 (base) + 3 (class) + 10 (free) = 18
- Other attributes: 5-8 depending on class bonuses

## PART 3: COMBAT LOOP

### Architecture: Cooldown-Based Real-Time (GCD + Priority)

Threshold uses a **Global Cooldown (GCD)** system with **individual ability cooldowns** layered on top. This is the WoW/FFXIV model adapted for an isometric browser MMO with the following constraints:

- **Tick rate:** 3 seconds (server synchronization interval for all regeneration, DoT/HoT effects, and periodic checks).
- **GCD:** 1.5 seconds. Every ability triggers the GCD. During the GCD, no other GCD-locked ability can be activated.
- **Off-GCD abilities:** Specific abilities (marked as "instant" or "reactive") can be used during the GCD window. These are limited and class-defining — they're the abilities that separate skilled play from button-mashing.
- **Individual cooldowns:** Each ability has its own cooldown independent of the GCD. A 15-second cooldown ability can only be used once every 15 seconds, but the GCD only prevents using *other* abilities for 1.5 seconds after activation.

### The Combat Rhythm

A typical combat turn looks like this:

```
0.0s  — Player activates Ability A (triggers GCD)
0.0s  — Ability A effect resolves
0.0s  — GCD begins (1.5s lockout)
0.7s  — Player activates Off-GCD Ability B (does not trigger GCD)
1.5s  — GCD ends
1.5s  — Player activates Ability C (triggers GCD)
1.5s  — Ability C effect resolves
3.0s  — Server tick: DoTs, HoTs, resource regen, periodic checks
3.0s  — GCD ends
3.0s  — Player activates Ability D...
```

At maximum efficiency, a player activates one GCD ability every 1.5 seconds and weaves off-GCD abilities between them. This produces a rhythm that rewards attention and timing without requiring twitch-level reflexes — critical for a browser MMO where input latency is higher than a native client.

### Resource Management

**Dual-resource system:** Physical abilities spend Stamina. Expression abilities spend Strand. Some abilities (particularly Advanced-tier) spend both.

**Resource tension by class:**

- **Sentinel:** Primarily Stamina-driven. Strand is a secondary pool for Warding and utility Expressions. The Sentinel's challenge is Stamina management during sustained encounters — burning through defensive abilities faster than regen can sustain.

- **Catalyst:** Primarily Strand-driven. Stamina is a secondary pool for movement abilities and emergency melee. The Catalyst's challenge is Strand conservation — their most powerful Expressions are expensive, and running dry means becoming a fragile body with no output.

- **Conduit:** Split resource management. Healing and Warding spend Strand. Cleansing and some buffs spend Stamina. The Conduit's challenge is managing *both* pools simultaneously while maintaining rotation rhythm — the hardest resource management in the game by design.

### Ability Types

Every ability falls into one of six categories that determine how it interacts with the combat loop:

**Instant** — Resolves immediately on activation. Triggers GCD unless marked off-GCD.

**Cast** — Requires a cast time (1.0s–3.0s) before resolving. Moving interrupts the cast. Triggers GCD on *completion*, not on initiation. High-risk, high-reward: cast abilities hit harder than instants but leave you vulnerable.

**Channel** — Delivers effects over a duration (2.0s–5.0s). Moving interrupts the channel. Unlike casts, channels deliver partial value if interrupted — a 3-second channel that's interrupted at 1.5 seconds delivers half its total effect.

**Reactive** — Can only be activated in response to a specific trigger (e.g., after being hit, after dodging, after an ally drops below a health threshold). Off-GCD by default. These create the moment-to-moment decision-making that separates skilled play from rotation-following.

**Toggle** — Active/inactive state. Consumes resources per tick while active. No GCD interaction. Examples: Threat Pulse (Defense), Chromatophore Fade (Stealth).

**Passive** — Always active once unlocked. No activation, no resource cost. Examples: Phenotypic Drift (Bioforming), Scarred Resilience (Cryptobiology).

### Auto-Attack

Auto-attack exists but is low-value. When a player is in range of a target and not activating abilities, they perform automatic basic attacks at a rate determined by weapon speed. Auto-attack damage is approximately 15-20% of total DPS at any given gear level — present enough to matter during GCD windows, weak enough that it never replaces ability usage.

```
Auto-Attack Damage = (Weapon Damage × 0.4) + (Soma × 0.5)
Auto-Attack Speed = Weapon Base Speed × (1 - Reflex × 0.005)
```

Auto-attacks can trigger on-hit effects from weapon coatings (Alchemy), passive abilities, and equipment procs.

### Threat and Aggro

Threshold uses a **threat table** system for PvE encounters:

```
Threat Generated = Damage Dealt × 1.0
Threat Generated (Healing) = Healing Done × 0.5
Threat Generated (Defense abilities) = Damage × Threat Multiplier (1.5x–3.0x)
```

- Sentinels generate bonus threat through Defense abilities (Threat Pulse, Calcified Response).
- Catalysts and Conduits generate reduced threat through passive modifiers.
- Threat decays at 2% per tick when not in combat with the source.
- Taunt effects (from Defense skill tree) force a target to attack the taunter for a duration, regardless of threat table position.

### Crowd Control Categories

CC effects fall into five categories. Each has a **diminishing returns** chain: the first application lasts full duration, the second lasts 50%, the third lasts 25%, and the fourth grants immunity for 15 seconds.

| Category | Effect | Example Abilities |
|----------|--------|-------------------|
| **Stun** | Cannot act | Excitotoxic Spike, Tetanic Lock |
| **Silence** | Cannot use Expression abilities | Neural Silence |
| **Root** | Cannot move, can still act | (future skill additions) |
| **Slow** | Movement speed reduced | Neurotoxic Strike, Cortisol Flood |
| **Disorient** | Random movement, cannot use targeted abilities | Groove Resonance (secondary) |

### Death and Recovery

When HP reaches 0:

1. **Cryptobiotic Tun check:** If the player has this ability (Cryptobiology 15), they enter suspended state instead of dying. Allies can revive them for 30 seconds before true death occurs.

2. **True death:** Player respawns at the nearest appropriate facility. Respawn location and penalties scale with civic status:

| Civic Status | Respawn Location | XP Loss | Resource Loss | Additional |
|-------------|------------------|---------|---------------|------------|
| Standard | Medical facility | 2% current level | 5% carried currency | None |
| Conditional | Underground clinic | 5% current level | 15% carried currency | +0.5 Directorate Suspicion |
| Authorized | Directorate medical bay | 1% current level | 2% carried currency | None |
| Suspended | Resistance safehouse | 8% current level | 25% carried currency | 5% chance of Expression Degradation (temporary -1 to random attribute for 1 hour) |

3. **Phlux variance on death:** High-Phlux characters experience variable death penalties (±Phlux × 0.5% on both XP and resource loss). A Phlux-15 character's XP loss on Conditional status could range from 2.5% to 7.5% — sometimes merciful, sometimes brutal.

### Combat Pacing Targets

| Encounter Type | Target Duration | Design Notes |
|----------------|-----------------|--------------|
| Trash mob (solo) | 8-15 seconds | Should feel efficient, not tedious. 4-8 GCDs. |
| Elite mob (solo) | 30-60 seconds | Requires resource management and cooldown planning. |
| Group trash pull | 15-25 seconds | AoE-friendly, Sentinel holds aggro, Catalyst cleaves. |
| Dungeon boss | 3-6 minutes | Full rotation cycles, phase transitions, mechanic checks. |
| World boss | 8-15 minutes | Multi-group coordination, Conduit rotation mastery required. |

## PART 4: CONDUIT ROTATION MECHANIC

### The Harmonic System

The Conduit's class identity is **constant cycling** — rotating between support functions rather than camping in one mode. This is implemented through the **Harmonic system**: a four-phase rotation that rewards rhythm and punishes stagnation.

### The Four Harmonics

At any given time, the Conduit is attuned to one of four Harmonics. Each Harmonic enhances a specific category of abilities:

| Harmonic | Enhancement | Color Signal |
|----------|------------|--------------|
| **Mend** | Healing abilities cost 20% less Strand and heal 15% more | Green pulse |
| **Ward** | Protective abilities gain 25% increased effect and duration | Blue pulse |
| **Amplify** | Ally buff abilities gain 20% potency, self-buff abilities gain 10% | Gold pulse |
| **Purge** | Cleansing and debuff abilities gain 30% effectiveness, AoE radius +20% | White pulse |

### Shifting Harmonics

Using an ability that matches your current Harmonic is efficient but does not advance the rotation. Using an ability from the **next Harmonic in sequence** triggers a **Harmonic Shift** — you move to the next phase and gain a brief **Resonance window** (3 seconds) during which all abilities gain a flat 10% bonus.

The sequence is fixed: **Mend → Ward → Amplify → Purge → Mend...**

```
Example rotation:
1. [MEND harmonic] Cast Bloodrush (healing, matches Mend — efficient but no shift)
2. [MEND harmonic] Cast Interferon Field (protective, matches Ward — SHIFT to Ward)
   → Resonance window opens (3s, all abilities +10%)
3. [WARD harmonic] Cast Interferon Field again (matches Ward — efficient)
4. [WARD harmonic] Cast Quorum Pulse (buff, matches Amplify — SHIFT to Amplify)
   → Resonance window opens
5. [AMPLIFY harmonic] Cast group buff (matches Amplify — efficient)
6. [AMPLIFY harmonic] Cast Chelation cleanse (cleanse, matches Purge — SHIFT to Purge)
   → Resonance window opens
7. [PURGE harmonic] Cleanse debuffs (efficient)
8. [PURGE harmonic] Cast Bloodrush (healing, matches Mend — SHIFT to Mend, full cycle complete)
   → FULL CYCLE BONUS: Resonance window extended to 5 seconds, all abilities +15%
```

### The Stagnation Penalty

If a Conduit stays in the same Harmonic for more than 12 seconds (4 ticks) without shifting, they enter **Dissonance** — a state where all abilities cost 15% more and heal/buff 10% less. Dissonance clears immediately on the next Harmonic Shift.

This is the stick that prevents camping. A Conduit who only heals is actively punished. A Conduit who rotates is actively rewarded. The skill expression comes from reading the fight and deciding *when* to shift — sometimes the group needs three Mend-phase heals before the Conduit can afford to move to Ward, and managing that tension against the Dissonance timer is the core gameplay challenge.

### Tempo Scaling

The Harmonic system scales with Resolve:

```
Resonance Window Duration = 3.0s + (Resolve × 0.1s)
Dissonance Timer = 12.0s + (Resolve × 0.3s)
Full Cycle Bonus Duration = 5.0s + (Resolve × 0.15s)
```

At 15 Resolve, the Resonance window is 4.5 seconds, the Dissonance timer is 16.5 seconds, and the Full Cycle Bonus lasts 7.25 seconds. This means high-Resolve Conduits have more breathing room in their rotation — still demanding, but with a wider margin for reading the fight.

### Reactive Harmonics (Off-GCD)

The Conduit has access to **Reactive Harmonic abilities** — off-GCD responses that trigger when specific conditions are met during a particular Harmonic:

| Harmonic | Trigger | Reactive Ability |
|----------|---------|------------------|
| Mend | Ally drops below 30% HP | **Triage** — Instant emergency heal (off-GCD, 30s cooldown) |
| Ward | Conduit takes damage exceeding 15% max HP in one hit | **Reflex Barrier** — Instant shield on self (off-GCD, 20s cooldown) |
| Amplify | Ally lands a critical hit | **Sympathetic Surge** — Instantly amplifies that ally's next ability by 25% (off-GCD, 25s cooldown) |
| Purge | Enemy applies a CC effect to an ally | **Cleansing Pulse** — Instantly removes the CC (off-GCD, 45s cooldown) |

These abilities are only available during their matching Harmonic, creating moments where the Conduit's rotation position directly determines their emergency response capability. A Conduit in Purge Harmonic who sees an ally drop to 30% HP cannot use Triage — they need to shift to Mend first, which means either burning a Ward-category ability to advance (skipping two phases) or accepting the delay. This creates genuine decision pressure that scales with encounter difficulty.

### Visual Feedback

The Conduit's current Harmonic is communicated through a persistent visual effect on the character sprite:

- **Mend:** Soft green luminescence along the arms and hands
- **Ward:** Blue-white geometric patterns across the torso
- **Amplify:** Gold filament traces along the spine and shoulders
- **Purge:** White radial pulse emanating from the chest

These are visible to all players, making the Conduit's current state readable by the group. A Sentinel who sees their Conduit in Amplify Harmonic knows to use their biggest ability now for the buff synergy.
